Our Heritage

Built in Sialkot, worn at every Pakistani wedding it touches

BinDawood Dulha House began as a single tailoring counter in Dara Arraian, built on the belief that a groom's wedding outfit deserves the same care as a bride's. Three generations later, the same hand-finishing techniques — zardozi, gota and dabka work — are still done by hand, panel by panel, rather than machine-replicated.

Every order that leaves our studio has been touched by a named tailor, fitted in a named session, and checked before it's pressed for delivery. That accountability, more than any single fabric or stitch, is what we mean by craftsmanship.
